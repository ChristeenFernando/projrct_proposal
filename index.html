    FAQ/Help

    Question 1: What is ethical hacking?

    Answer: Ethical hacking, also known as white-hat hacking, is the practice of intentionally probing computer systems, networks, or applications for security vulnerabilities. Ethical hackers do this to identify and fix potential security weaknesses before malicious hackers can exploit them.

    Question 2: How can I get started with ethical hacking?

    Answer: To get started with ethical hacking, you can learn about computer networks, security concepts, and programming languages. Consider obtaining certifications like Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) or CompTIA Security+ to enhance your skills and credibility.

    Question 3: What tools are commonly used by ethical hackers?

    Answer: Ethical hackers often use a variety of tools, including network scanners, vulnerability assessment tools, password crackers, and penetration testing frameworks. Some popular tools include Nmap, Wireshark, Metasploit, and John the Ripper.
